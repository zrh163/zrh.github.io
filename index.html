  • 视觉与交互设计工具：Figma，用于搭建网页原型、设计界面视觉风格（如“深蓝+米白”配色、字体层级），支持实时协作与响应式布局预览，确保在电脑、平板、手机端均有良好显示效果。
  • 开发辅助工具：Visual Studio Code（搭配HTML/CSS/JavaScript插件），用于编写网页代码并调试交互功能；Canva，用于设计网页头部Banner（如法庭徽章图标），提升视觉辨识度。
  • 内容整理工具：Notion，用于梳理《Gideon v. Wainwright》案的核心要素、双语术语，确保信息分类清晰，为网页排版提供逻辑依据。

设计方法

  • 用户中心设计法：以“法律学习者”为核心用户，优先满足“快速定位案例关键信息”“高效记忆法律双语术语”需求——例如将“法庭意见书要素”设为核心模块，用“分点+加粗”突出关键内容；双语知识库用表格呈现，方便中英文对比。
  • 信息分层法：按“核心信息（一级标题）-辅助信息（二级标题）-拓展信息（案例语境）”划分优先级，一级标题用深蓝加粗字体，二级标题用中蓝字体，术语用蓝色标注并添加hover提示，降低阅读成本。
  • 交互优化法：添加“顶部固定导航栏”，点击即可跳转至对应模块；在双语知识库的英文术语上添加“hover提示”，鼠标悬浮时显示中文释义+案例原文，增强学习便利性。

设计过程

  1. 需求分析与规划（1天）：梳理用户需求（呈现案例要素、提供双语学习资源），绘制思维导图确定三大核心模块，明确“法庭意见书要素”需严格基于《Gideon v. Wainwright》案原文提取，双语知识库需匹配案例高频法律术语。
  2. 原型与视觉设计（2天）：在Figma中搭建网页框架，确定主色调为“深蓝（#0f3057）+米白（#f8f9fa）”（深蓝象征法律严谨性，米白提升阅读舒适度），字体选用“思源黑体（中文）+Times New Roman（英文）”，确保中英文显示协调。
  3. 代码实现与交互开发（2天）：用HTML搭建网页结构，CSS实现视觉样式（如导航栏固定、表格hover变色），JavaScript逻辑嵌入CSS（术语hover提示）；插入整理好的案例内容，反复测试文字格式与交互功能。
  4. 测试与优化（1天）：在Chrome、Edge浏览器及手机端测试网页显示，修复响应式布局错位问题；邀请2名法律专业学生试用，根据“术语释义准确性”“导航便捷性”反馈调整内容，最终完成网页。

（二）法庭意见书要素（英文）

1. Nature of Case

A federal habeas corpus petition case, in which the petitioner (Gideon) challenged his conviction and sentence by the Florida state court. The core dispute lies in whether the state court’s refusal to appoint counsel for an indigent defendant (Gideon) violates the U.S. Constitution, ultimately leading to the U.S. Supreme Court reconsidering and overruling the prior precedent Betts v. Brady.

2. Concise Rule of Law

The Sixth Amendment’s guarantee of the right to counsel for indigent criminal defendants in federal courts is a fundamental right essential to a fair trial. This right is made obligatory upon state courts through the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment, meaning states must appoint counsel for indigent defendants charged with felonies (and other serious crimes) in state court proceedings.

3. Facts

  • Gideon was charged with breaking and entering a poolroom with intent to commit a misdemeanor (a felony under Florida law) in a Florida state court.
  • Appearing in court without funds or a lawyer, Gideon requested the court to appoint counsel for him, but the request was denied. The Florida court stated that state law only allowed counsel appointment for defendants charged with capital offenses.
  • Gideon conducted his own defense as a layman: he made an opening statement, cross-examined the state’s witnesses, presented his own witnesses, declined to testify himself, and argued his innocence.
  • The jury returned a verdict of guilty, and Gideon was sentenced to five years in state prison.
  • Gideon later filed a habeas corpus petition in the Florida Supreme Court, claiming the trial court’s refusal to appoint counsel denied him rights guaranteed by the U.S. Constitution and the Bill of Rights. The Florida Supreme Court denied relief without an opinion.
  • Gideon then petitioned the U.S. Supreme Court for certiorari, which granted the request and appointed counsel to represent him, asking both sides to discuss whether Betts v. Brady should be reconsidered.

4. Issue

Whether the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment requires state courts to appoint counsel for indigent defendants charged with felonies (non-capital offenses) in state criminal proceedings, thereby making the Sixth Amendment’s right to counsel applicable to the states.

5. Holding

  • The Sixth Amendment’s guarantee of counsel is a fundamental right essential to a fair trial. This right is incorporated into the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment and thus binding on state courts.
  • State courts must appoint counsel for indigent defendants charged with felonies (and other serious crimes) in state criminal trials. The Florida state court’s refusal to appoint counsel for Gideon violated his constitutional rights.
  • The prior precedent Betts v. Brady (which held that appointment of counsel for indigent non-capital defendants in state courts was not required unless “special circumstances” existed) was overruled, as it conflicted with fundamental constitutional principles and prior precedents (e.g., Powell v. Alabama).

6. Decision

The U.S. Supreme Court reversed the judgment of the Florida Supreme Court, holding that the trial court’s refusal to appoint counsel for Gideon violated the Due Process Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. Gideon’s conviction was vacated, and he was entitled to a new trial with appointed counsel.

三、典型句型（5个）

英文句型 中文释义 案例语境示例 学习要点
[Court] held that [legal rule], thereby [legal consequence]. [法院]认为[法律规则]，因此产生[法律后果]。 The Supreme Court held that the Sixth Amendment’s right to counsel is fundamental, thereby applying it to states.（最高法院认为第六修正案的律师权具有根本性，因此将其适用于各州。） 判决书“holding”部分核心句型，前半部分表述裁判规则，后半部分用“thereby”衔接法律后果，逻辑严谨。
[Petitioner] challenged [judgment] on the ground that [legal claim]. [申请人]以[法律主张]为依据，对[判决]提出异议。 Gideon challenged his conviction on the ground that his right to counsel was violated.（吉迪恩以律师权被侵犯为依据，对定罪提出异议。） 用于描述“当事人异议理由”，“on the ground that”后需明确具体法律依据，是“facts”模块常用句型。
[Precedent] is overruled because it conflicts with [principle/precedent]. [先例]因与[原则/先例]冲突而被推翻。 Betts v. Brady is overruled because it conflicts with fundamental constitutional principles.（《贝茨诉布雷迪案》因与基本宪法原则冲突而被推翻。） 体现判例法“修正先例”的逻辑，需清晰说明“被推翻对象”与“冲突依据”，常见于“holding”部分。
[Amicus curiae] filed a brief urging [action] on the basis of [argument]. [法庭之友]基于[论点]提交意见书，主张[行动]。 The ACLU filed a brief urging reversal on the basis of indigent defendants’ right to counsel.（美国公民自由联盟基于贫困被告人律师权，提交意见书主张撤销原判。） 描述第三方参与诉讼的方式，“urging”后接法院行动，“on the basis of”后接核心论点，是“facts”模块关键句型。
The [Amendment] incorporates [right] into the Due Process Clause, binding [states]. [修正案]将[权利]纳入正当程序条款，对[各州]具有约束力。 The Sixth Amendment incorporates the right to counsel into the Due Process Clause, binding state courts.（第六修正案将律师权纳入正当程序条款，对州法院具有约束力。） 解释“联邦权利适用于各州”的宪法逻辑，是“rule of law”部分核心句型，需明确“权利-修正案-约束对象”。
